LED Fade - discussion


 
 
 

 
LED Fade
Light Emitting Diode
 		Fade an external light emitting diode (LED), by writing an             'analog' value.

This example shows how you can change the brightness of your LED, instead of just turning it 'on' or 'off' as we did in "LED Blink". Remember, to turn the LED 'on' or 'off', we wrote a HIGH or a LOW value to a pin (corresponding to 5V and 0V respectively). Now, we will write to the pin values that are in between HIGH and LOW, using the function analogWrite(). This functions works as follows: you give it a number between 0 and 255, where 0 corresponds to LOW (0V), 255 corresponds to HIGH (5V and the numbers in between result in voltages between LOW and HIGH. It is important to know that the analogWrite() function only works with pins that have a '~' symbol in front of the number shown on the Arduino board.

Advanced (this explanation is optional): You can think of analogWrite() as a function that allows you to give a pin voltage values between 0V and 5V, as explained above. However, how it is actually implemented is a little different. The pin voltage doesn't actually take on a voltage between 0V and 5V, such as 1.6V. Instead, it will only be either 0V (LOW) or 5V (HIGH) and jump between those two values very quickly, such the average value is 1.6V (in this case, it would be 5V for 32% of the time and 0V for 68% of the time). This kind of programmable HIGH-LOW signal is called PWM or Pulse Width Modulation. Only certain pins on the board can generate PWM and thus support analogWrite(). These pins have a '~' symbol in front of the number. This is the reason why we used pin 9 in our example here. For more info on analogWrite(), you can check out https://www.arduino.cc/en/Reference/AnalogWrite.
